# QF-008 — Discovery Methodology

**Document ID:** QF-008

**Version:** 1.0.0

**Status:** Active

**Owner:** QIRA

**Last Updated:** 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official Discovery Methodology used by QIRA.

Discovery is the foundation of every engagement.

QIRA does not begin with software.

QIRA begins with understanding the customer's business.

Every implementation must follow this methodology.

---

# Discovery Philosophy

Traditional software vendors ask:

> "What system do you need?"

QIRA asks:

> "What business outcome are you trying to achieve?"

Software is never the objective.

Business transformation is the objective.

---

# Primary Goals

Every Discovery should achieve the following objectives:

- Understand the customer's business.
- Understand current workflows.
- Identify business pain points.
- Identify improvement opportunities.
- Measure digital maturity.
- Measure AI readiness.
- Define success criteria.
- Recommend an implementation roadmap.

---

# Discovery Principles

Discovery is:

- Collaborative
- Evidence-based
- Business-first
- AI-assisted
- Outcome-oriented
- Repeatable
- Structured

Discovery is NOT:

- A sales pitch
- A product demonstration
- A questionnaire
- A software requirement meeting

---

# Phase 1 — Lead Qualification

Purpose

Determine whether the organization is a good fit for QIRA.

Information collected

- Industry
- Company Size
- Number of Employees
- Existing Systems
- Current Challenges
- Budget Expectations
- Timeline
- Decision Makers

Deliverable

Lead Qualification Score

---

# Phase 2 — Business Alignment

Purpose

Understand the customer's strategic objectives.

Primary Question

> If this project is successful 12 months from now,
> what will have changed in your business?

Topics

- Business Goals
- Revenue Goals
- Customer Experience
- Operational Goals
- Growth Plans
- Digital Strategy

Deliverable

Business Alignment Statement

---

# Phase 3 — Discovery Planning

Purpose

Prepare the Discovery process.

Activities

- Define stakeholders.
- Define interview schedule.
- Request required documents.
- Configure Discovery Workspace.
- Assign AI Employees.

Deliverable

Discovery Plan

---

# Phase 4 — AI Interview

Purpose

Collect business information through natural conversation.

AI Interview should feel like a consultant.

Never like a form.

AI may ask follow-up questions.

Topics include:

- Business Model
- Sales
- Marketing
- Customer Service
- Operations
- Finance
- Human Resources
- IT
- Compliance
- Reporting

Deliverable

Discovery Transcript

---

# Phase 5 — Document Collection

Purpose

Collect organizational knowledge.

Examples

- Company Profile
- Organization Chart
- SOP
- Existing Systems
- Product Catalog
- Service Catalog
- Reports
- Forms
- Policies
- Contracts
- Website
- Marketing Materials

All documents become searchable inside the Discovery Workspace.

---

# Phase 6 — Business Analysis

Purpose

Transform raw information into insights.

Performed by

AI Business Analyst

Reviewed by

Human Consultant

Outputs

- Pain Points
- Opportunities
- Risks
- Bottlenecks
- Duplicate Processes
- Manual Activities
- Automation Candidates
- AI Opportunities

---

# Phase 7 — Solution Mapping

Purpose

Map business problems to QIRA capabilities.

Outputs

Recommended

- AI Employees
- Automation
- Dashboards
- Workflows
- Integrations
- Web Applications
- Knowledge Systems

Deliverable

Solution Map

---

# Phase 8 — Proposal

Proposal should contain

- Executive Summary
- Business Goals
- Current State
- Recommended Solution
- Architecture
- Timeline
- Deliverables
- Investment
- Implementation Plan

Proposal is reviewed by a Human Consultant before delivery.

# Success Metrics

A successful Discovery produces:

- Clear Business Objectives
- Shared Understanding
- Accurate Current-State Analysis
- Prioritized Opportunities
- Practical Roadmap
- Executive Buy-In
- High-Quality Proposal

---

# Discovery Rules

Every Discovery must:

- Start with business outcomes.
- Use AI as an assistant.
- Preserve customer knowledge.
- Avoid assumptions.
- Validate findings.
- Document everything.
- Respect confidentiality.

---

# Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- Jumping directly into software discussions.
- Asking unnecessary questions.
- Duplicating interviews.
- Making technical assumptions.
- Designing solutions before understanding the business.
- Collecting information without purpose.
